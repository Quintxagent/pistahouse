Our Story

From One Kitchen in the Old City, to a Name the World Knows

It began in 1997 as a modest family kitchen in Shalibanda, in the heart of Hyderabad's Old City, started by Mohammed Abdul Majeed with one simple goal — to share the true flavours of Hyderabadi cooking with his city.

Nearly three decades on, that single kitchen has grown into one of the most recognised food names in India — celebrated for a Haleem so consistent and so loved that it travels across continents every Ramzan, and for a Biryani that's still cooked the traditional dum way, sealed and steamed, exactly as it always has been.

Every dish leaving a Pista House kitchen today still follows that founding promise: quality first, tradition always.
